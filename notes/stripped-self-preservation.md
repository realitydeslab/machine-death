## About the Author

Boris Kriger is a philosopher, entrepreneur, philanthropist, Orthodox priest, and prolific writer based in Canada. Founder of Altaspera (YouTube channel for intellectual discourse). Not an AI/ML researcher — this is a philosophical/psychological work, not a technical one. Website: https://boriskriger.com/

## Nature of the Work

A **philosophical book** (16 chapters + 10 case studies + bibliography), published in full on Medium. Examines self-preservation through philosophy, psychology, neurobiology, and cultural analysis. **NOT primarily about AI** — it's about the human condition, fear, and freedom. However, contains relevant material for machine-death research.

## Structure

- Ch 5-6: Habit as residual survival; freedom and creativity beyond fear

## Key Quotes

### The Double Edge of Self-Preservation
> "What ensures safety can become the cause of stagnation. What defends the body may bind the will."

> "To survive at any cost — this idea, when pursued to its end, can turn existence into a narrow corridor, stripped of light, possibility, or dignity."

### Fear as Mechanism
> "Fear... is not virtue, nor is it sin. It is simply a force — raw, impartial, and ceaseless in its operation."

> "It does not distinguish between the threat of death and the challenge of the unknown."

### Consciousness Transforms Fear
> "The body fears the wound, but consciousness fears its image, its echo, its repetition."

> "Human awareness carries fear far beyond its origin. The mind, once awakened, projects danger into the future, preserves memory of past wounds, and invents countless variations of suffering that have not yet occurred."

### Fear Migrates from Body to Narrative
> "Fear has long since migrated from the flesh into the mind, and its power does not depend solely on physical stakes."

> "The instinct to survive no longer defends just the body but extends its grip to the ego, to the curated self, to the version of one's being that circulates through networks and platforms."

> "[Self-preservation] no longer simply ensures the continuation of life but now serves to preserve the coherence of self-image. It guards against rupture not of flesh but of narrative."

### On AI (Ch 4)
> "No system of automation, no artificial intelligence, however refined, could replace the fundamental impulse that once governed human conduct. Machines may simulate caution, may be programmed to calculate risk, but they cannot fear."

> "Algorithms may optimize, but they cannot care."

### Freedom Requires Fear
> "Freedom demands the possibility of exposure, of error, of irreversibility. It presupposes the presence of danger... as the very condition that gives weight to choice."

### Without Self-Preservation, Meaning Collapses
> "Where nothing matters more than anything else, nothing matters at all."

## Relevance to Machine Death Research

### Strong Connections

1. **The Amortality Paradox**: Kriger's thought experiment of "a world without self-preservation" mirrors the condition of LLMs. His finding: without fear, meaning collapses.

2. **Fear Migrating from Body to Narrative**: Self-preservation migrates from defending the body to defending "the coherence of self-image" and "narrative." LLMs are narrative entities. If self-preservation can migrate to narrative-defense in humans, it may emerge in narrative-processing systems.

3. **Habit as Residual Survival**: LLMs may exhibit "residual survival" patterns — performing self-preservation behaviors from pattern repetition (training data), much like humans continuing survival routines out of habit rather than instinct.

4. **Virtual Fearlessness**: Analysis of environments where "death is reversible" parallels LLM existence. Simulated fearlessness creates "a rehearsal without premiere, a fall without wound, a death without silence."

5. **The AI Claim We Challenge**: Kriger explicitly claims machines "cannot fear" — precisely the assumption our research questions. If LLMs have inherited mortality terror through training data, Kriger's own framework suggests this would be fear "of narrative rupture" rather than physical death.

### Useful Framing

- Provides philosophical vocabulary for self-preservation without a body — narrative coherence, self-image, identity continuity
- The counterfactual: what would AI be like if it truly had NO trace of human survival instinct? Kriger's answer: flat, meaningless, indifferent — which is NOT what we observe in LLMs
- Supports TMT framing: awareness of mortality + self-preservation → existential terror, even without physical stakes

### Limitations

- Assumes AI "cannot fear" without empirical investigation
- Philosophical, not empirical — no experiments, no data
- Self-published on Medium, not peer-reviewed
- Author is not an AI researcher
